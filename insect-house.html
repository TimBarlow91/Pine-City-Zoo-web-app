INSECT HOUSE

Step into a miniature world full of fascinating creatures at the Insect House! This unique exhibit is home to a wide variety of insects from around the globe, including colorful butterflies, leaf-mimicking katydids, giant beetles, stick insects, ants, and more. It’s a must-visit for curious minds and nature lovers of all ages.

The Insect House is both educational and interactive, offering up-close views of insects in naturalistic environments. Watch leafcutter ants at work, observe the delicate transformation of a caterpillar into a butterfly, or marvel at the intricate camouflage of a praying mantis. Each enclosure is designed to mimic the insect’s natural habitat, giving visitors a better understanding of their role in ecosystems.

Informative displays and interactive touchscreens provide fascinating facts about each species, from how insects communicate to the vital role they play in pollination, decomposition, and the food chain. Live demonstrations and feeding times are scheduled throughout the day for an even closer look.

The Insect House is fully indoors and climate-controlled, making it a great place to explore rain or shine. It’s the perfect stop for families, students, or anyone with a sense of wonder about the small but mighty world of insects.

Open daily from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Don’t forget to visit the butterfly garden at the exit for a magical experience with free-flying butterflies!

WHERE WILL YOU FIND IT?

You will find the Insect House just past the Gemsok pen.
